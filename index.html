Nilanjan Goswami, Ph.D.

Graphics Architect @ Facebook


Nilanjan is a Graphics Architect at the Facebook (Menlo Park, CA). He works in the area of 3D graphics pipeline architecture optimization for advanced rendering techniques, virtual reality and augmented reality. He has years of experience in working on tile based and immediate mode computer graphics rendering techniques for low power, high efficiency mobile graphics pipelines. During the years of industry experience, he also worked on image, audio, and video processing.

He has graduated with Ph.D. in Computer Architecture from the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering in the University of Florida. His research interest includes architecture for deep learning, virtual/augmented reality, computer graphics, etc.

Interest/Expertise

  • Architectures for Augmented/Virtual reality devices.
  • Tile-based / Multi-pass / Immediate-mode graphics rendering hardware architecture (TBDR / TBMR / IMR).
  • Hidden surface removal algorithm in TBDR/TBMR/IMR hardware rendering pipeline.
  • Compression techniques for various graphics data structures.
  • Fragment interpolator architecture optimization for various graphics hardwares.
  • Vertex processing optimization including but not limited to primitive culling, attribute fetch optimization, binning.
  • Game engine architecture (ECS, semantic graph based design).
